How to support someone who's been raped

  • Recognising the different behaviours they may exhibit; hyper sexuality, or desiring no sex at all, they may even be more afraid of sexual interactions and closeness.
  • If they choose to disclose this to you, let them know:
  • That you can take them to the police station to make a report.
  • You can call rape services to get them support to talk to someone who can advise and support them through this.
  • You'll be there for them whenever they need.
  • If they need space, still maintain contact to show them you are there and that you care about their well being.
  • Help them talk to people who have also suffered through something similar, so they can hear how other survivors were able to move forward with their lives.

How to Report

Take them to the police station with any evidence they may have of the incident as this will make their claim stronger. If they have told you about the incident days after it happened this may affect the results of the case, however do still encourage them to report as this may help create a stronger case against the abuser especially if this brings up any previous convictions they may have. If you do not feel comfortable leaving the house and making the report just call 999 and tell them you would like to report a sexual abuse case and they will come to your house and start the case.

You can also call the Havens and they can help you write a case to help get the police involved.
